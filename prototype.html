WhatsApp

Albus Dumbledore – “I want to be able to contact my friends overseas, but without paying. I've never used WhatsApp, is there something out there to teach me?”

Background

- Old School professor

- Refuses to get with the times, but is regrettably feeling the repercussions of not adapting

- Loves to send messages to friends overseas through mail, but hates such long responses

Needs and Goals

- Contact friends overseas with little time delay

- Be able to potentially communicate to colleagues in group formats

- Learn to adapt to technological features

Skills and Experience

- Great Communicative skills

- Little to no experience with technology

Context Scenario

Albus Dumbledore has always had a pen pal, as long as he can remember. He loves learning about the traditions and events that go on in someone else's life halfway across the world. He originally appreciated the time it took for his pen pals to get back to him, however, now with COVID-19 ruining air travel and delaying packages, he doesn't want to wait an incredibly long amount of time for a reply. His pen pal suggested he use WhatsApp so they can communicate more frequently in his last mail, so Albus decides to take him up on that offer. However, Albus has no idea how to use WhatsApp. He decides to try and use the in-app instructions but finds that it is difficult to understand and doesn’t provide him with enough information on how to use the app, so he decides to google the best way to learn how to use WhatsApp. As many have suggested with high regard, BoomerLifeSupport is one of the best ways to be taught about fundamentals on an app such as the likes of WhatsApp. Albus acknowledges this and decides to download the app BoomerLifeSupport. Albus opens up BoomerLifeSupport, which then links him to WhatsApp, immediately displaying an overlay on top of the app, encouraging him to click on features/buttons. As Albus clicks on a specific button, BoomerLifeSupport gives him a thorough yet simple explanation of the function. Through BoomerLifeSupport, Albus is now feeling more confident using the app, and decides that he can now directly message his pen pal!

Google Maps

Hubert – “I want to be able to use google maps for the first time without having technical issues”

Background

- An elderly male, lives with his wife in an outdated home, relying on habits and instinct rather than technology.

- Loves visiting new places (loves driving)

Needs and Goals

- An easy way to find new places and locations

- Quick ways to find directions on how to get to these places

- Wants to replace the Melways as it doesn’t have the fastest routes due to lack of updates

Skills and Experience

- Great sense of direction

- Good communication skills and can follow instructions well

- Lack of experience with technology, very limited due to being frustrated easily (however, wants to learn)

Context Scenario

It is the beginning of the week again, Hubert’s dedicated time of the week to find a new spot him and his wife Stella can enjoy a nice sunset, a lookout! He seemed to have extensively looked through his Melways to find a new location, to much of his nobody’s surprise, it is an outdated edition and does not have any new locations. So, he decides to go to one of his very first lookouts in Braybrook, a place he has not been in a while. He noticed that there has been a lot of roadworks in Braybrook and therefore does not know any of the new roads and believes that it could potentially have a faster way to get to his special spot. Since Melways hasn’t been updated, he decides to try and use Google Maps, but isn’t too sure on how it works. He then realises that he has Boomer Life Support to quickly help him guide through the functions of the app! He opens up BLS to assist him in manoeuvring google maps. BLS opens up google maps and points to the search bar on the top of his screen. He selects it and puts in the address of his destination. Hubert is then instructed to turn on his location by swiping down on the notifications screen. He does so and the app begins navigating him from his phone.

Facebook

Susan - “Why are people making a joke out of face masks during a flu pandemic? I should keep my friends informed on the importance of face masks so we can all go back to our old reality!”

Background

- A social butterfly that recently discovered Facebook

- Susan is a retired nurse.

Needs and Goals

- Susan wants to be able to keep in contact with her friends.

- Wants to keep her friends posted on the latest safety precautions of COVID-19.

Skills and Experience

- Susan was recently gifted a new iPad for her 70th birthday.

- Has an outdated iPhone that she uses to make phone calls and text messages.

Context Scenario

As a retired nurse and previous experience in the healthcare sector, Susan finds it important to keep her friends updated on the importance of taking necessary precautions for COVID-19. Recently, the Victorian government made face masks mandatory for everyone when out in public. However, there have been anti masks protests and a large number of people that do not believe in the importance of face masks. Susan wants to write a Facebook post and keep her friends well informed about the role face masks play in helping us keep our statistics down. Susan opens the Facebook app on her new iPad, however there are too many features and she finds it hard to navigate around the app. Susan stumbles across Boomer Life Support on the AppStore and decides to download it. Boomer Life Support opens up Facebook and with overlay instructions, Susan is now able to navigate through the app. An arrow points to the "What's on your mind, Susan" bar, Susan now knows how to navigate and create a new post and is able to share important information with her friends.

Outlook

Abe - “I want to send an email to my doctor because i can’t see him during this pandemic.”

Background

- Abe is a retired engineer and now lives in a retirement home.

- Enjoys doing TikTok dances with his grandchildren.

Needs and Goals

- Abe needs to email his doctor to ask him for a new prescription of his medication. Usually, Abe would go to the clinic to speak to DR. Hibbert in person, but because of the ongoing pandemic, Abe is forced to contact the doctor via online resources. Abe has been told to email his doctor his requirements, so he needs help figuring out how to send an email to DR. Hibbert via his iPad.

Skills and Experience

- Abe can easily navigate through simple mobile applications. He knows how to use Facebook, Skype and Youtube.

- A pretty tech savvy senior, he owns an iPhone and an iPad.

Context Scenario

Abe is sitting in his retirement home and opens up his ipad to Boomer Life Support. Abe has used Boomer Life Support in the past to figure out how to use Facebook, so once again, Abe turns to the app with assistance in learning how to email his doctor. Abe opens up Boomer Life support and clicks on the outlook button, and selects ‘composing emails’ from the list of helpful tips Boomer Life Support provides. The app explains to Abe how he can compose an email and send it to his doctor. The overlay appears over outlook and Abe is shown with clear and concise instructions on how to email DR. Hibbert. Abe is taught to add a subject field and how to attach any necessary attachments to the email. Today, Abe is just emailing his doctor to provide him with a new prescription. Abe composes his email and hits send.

Centrelink

Hubert Farnsworth’s business has recently been forced to close due to the pandemic. Hubert is eligible for government funding to assist him with costs of living as well paying his employees. Hubert has to upload documents via the Centrelink app.

Background

- A retired scientist but still works on projects and experiments from time to time.

- Has multiple employees who will be eligible for JobKeeper payments.

Needs and Goals

- Due to restrictions in place for COVID-19, Hubert must provide relevant documents to centrelink to be approved for financial support.

Skills and Experience

- Enjoys learning new things.

- Very intelligent but has doesnt use his phone often, therefore unsure how to use it.

- Used to be a professor.

Context Scenario

Hubert Farnsworth has just been informed that his business will not be eligible to run during the stage 4 lockdown. Huberts business has taken a big hit to profits in light of the news. Hubert has to apply for JobKeeper payments so all of his employees can still get paid as well as apply for his own funding. Hubert is required to upload relevant documents of his employees and his business to the Centrelink app. Hubert almost never uses his phone, so he is unsure of how to navigate the centrelink app. Hubert downloads the Boomer Life Support app and clicks on the centrelink assistance option. Boomer Life Support opens up his centrelink app and an overlay appears that shows Hubert how to upload his documents. Hubert follows the steps and successfully applies for the funding.
