Project Motivation

Describe what motivated your group to choose this particular project?

There were contributing factors that helped motivate this group to develop ‘Boomer Life Support’. In a time where we are currently restricted to our homes, communication between family members and friends has become harder than ever. Although, through the use of technology, it has been possible to continue our friendships and relationships through an online world. However, being an elderly person means that they might not be super confident or aware of the technology they have access to, and thus any knowledge of how to use it. So it was our intention as a group to try and develop an app that could explain to elderly people how to use the wonders of the tech world without having to actually be there in person. E.g. visiting grandparents to explain how to message online through social media.

Another motivator was the fact that two 2 members work in a retail tech store. Throughout these shifts, the two members are constantly approached by elderly people asking how to complete a certain task on their phone. Some of these questions include; ‘How do I open up Facebook?’, ‘How do I create an Email?’, ‘How do I reset my password?’. As a millennial, questions like this are easy to answer, however, it does get frustrating and annoying through constant questions. Therefore, we decided to develop an app that helps to answer these questions for them!

How does it relate to your individual and collective interests? It is a laudable aim to try and create something which is beyond your current knowledge level and which addresses areas where you can enrich your IT Skills.

This project motivates me because it focuses on the reason why I wanted to study IT, to help other people. Not only would this allow the elderly to learn technology and make their lives easier.

At the current skill level, we are still all quite new to HTML5 and CSS3, with an exception for the very basic functions. So in terms of developing a whole website, and promoting how our application will function will be a whole new challenge in itself. I definitely believe developing something beyond our skill set is a laudable aim. We all have different strengths and weaknesses, and therefore if we all learn off of each other, we can start to develop more skills. Some say being thrown into the deep end is a fast way to learn, we totally agree. Having to create a website from scratch, with the added functionality of CSS3 and JavaScript will help to develop our skills as web programmers, and hopefully spark some further interest into jobs in that field.
